BENGOUGH, John Wilson, 1851-1923.

THE/UP-TO-DATE/ [pict. ornament] /PRIMER:/A/FIRST BOOK OF LESSONS/FOR/LITTLE POLITICAL ECONOMISTS,/IN WORDS OF ONE SYLLABLE/WITH PICTURES/BY/J.W. BENGOUGH/Designed for use in Schools, Colleges, Universities and other/Seats of Learning/NEW YORK/FUNK & WAGNALLS COMPANY/LONDON AND TORONTO/1896/ [lines 1-7 in ornamental lettering].
Half-title. c. 1895 Funk & Wagnalls Co. 1-75 pp. 182x124 mm. Text illus.
Light brown stiff wrappers. Front bears title, sub-title, pict. ornament & author within a dbl. border; back bears humorous, fictitious comments within a dbl. border.
F.f.l. n.p. 1895 (NUC).

Bengough was a cartoonist and a poet, born at Toronto, Ontario. In 1873 he founded Grip, a humorous weekly published in Toronto, and his cartoons in this journal gained a wide audience. Later he became a cartoonist with the Montreal Star and then with the Toronto Globe. He was an advocate of the single tax, prohibition and free trade. (Macmillan). Most of his political ideas are contained in his Up-to-date primer. The following is an extract from Lesson XI: 'But look at these Two! Are they not both Men? They are. Yet one is Fat and Rich, and one is Lean and Poor! How comes this? It must be that the Fat one has a will to Work, and so by his Toil on the Land gets all the Good Things he needs, while the Lean one will not Work and so must needs Starve and go in Rags. So it would seem, my Child; but things are not as they seem.'
